Example sentence:They have never evinced any readiness or ability to negotiate.
corrugated [adj.] [ˈkɒrəgeɪtɪd] / wrinkled [adj.]
Word explanation:folded into furrows and ridges; wrinkled
Example sentence:His brow was corrugated with worry.
flaunt [v.] [n.] [flɔːnt] / show off
Word explanation:to show or make obvious something you are proud of in order to get admiration
Example sentence:He’s very rich, but he doesn’t like to flaunt his wealth or waste his money.
manageable [adj.] [ˈmænɪdʒəbl] / controllable / governable
Word explanation:easy or possible to deal with
Example sentence:They divided the students into three manageable groups.
grandiose [adj.] [ˈgrændiəʊs] -> grandiose plans
Word explanation:larger and containing more detail than necessary, or intended to seem important or great
Example sentence:The grandiose scheme for a journey across the desert came to nothing.
treacherous [adj.] [ˈtretʃərəs] / disloyal / faithless
Word explanation:a person who is treacherous deceives someone who trusts them, or has no loyalty
Example sentence:A known enemy is better than a treacherous friend.
